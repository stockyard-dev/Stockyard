Stockyard vs Helicone

Helicone is a managed observability layer for LLM apps. Stockyard is a self-hosted platform that includes observability alongside five other integrated apps.

When to choose Helicone

Helicone is the right choice if observability is your primary need and you want a polished, managed SaaS with zero ops overhead. Their dashboards are excellent, their team ships fast, and they have strong integrations with the broader LLM tooling ecosystem. If you don’t want to self-host anything, Helicone is a great option.

When to choose Stockyard

Stockyard is the right choice if you want observability and everything else in one place. It includes a full proxy with 70 middleware modules, an audit ledger, prompt management, a workflow engine, and a config marketplace. If you’re evaluating OpenAI alternatives, Stockyard gives you the whole stack self-hosted — your data never leaves your infrastructure.

If compliance, data residency, or cost control matter to your team, self-hosting a single binary is simpler and cheaper than subscribing to multiple managed services.

The bottom line

Helicone is excellent at observability. Stockyard gives you that same capability plus a full proxy, audit system, prompt studio, workflow engine, and marketplace — all self-hosted. If you only need logging and dashboards, Helicone is great. If you want the whole platform, Stockyard is one binary.
