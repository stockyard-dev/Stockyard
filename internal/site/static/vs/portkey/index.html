Stockyard vs Portkey

Portkey is a managed AI gateway with observability and guardrails. Stockyard is a self-hosted platform that bundles the gateway with five more apps.

When to choose Portkey

Portkey is the right choice if you want a managed AI gateway with a mature enterprise offering. They have SOC2 compliance, dedicated support, and a polished product that many teams use in production. If your team prefers managed services and needs enterprise support contracts, Portkey delivers.

When to choose Stockyard

Stockyard is the right choice if you want the full platform self-hosted. Stockyard gives you everything Portkey offers — gateway, observability, guardrails, prompt management — plus a tamper-evident audit ledger, a DAG workflow engine, and a config marketplace. All in a single binary with no cloud dependency.

At $9.99/month for unlimited self-hosted usage vs. usage-based pricing, the cost difference grows quickly at scale. And with the full source available, you’re never locked in.

The bottom line

Portkey is a polished managed gateway. Stockyard gives you that same gateway plus workflows, audit trails, and a marketplace — fully self-hosted. If you want managed enterprise with SOC2, Portkey delivers. If you want ownership, flexibility, and more platform surface area, Stockyard is the binary.
